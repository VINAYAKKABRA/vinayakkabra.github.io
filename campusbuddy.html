Campus Buddy

                             Make friends on campus

I chose to take CS 522: Human-Computer Interaction during my third semester at the University of Illinois, Chicago (UIC). “Campus Buddy” was our final project for the course. We study several research articles during the course on a variety of subjects, including “Color,” “Graphic Space,” “Conceptual Models,” etc. We attempted to include new ideas and terminology, such as “meta-data,” “annotations,” etc., into our project.

INTRODUCTION

Campus Buddy aims to undo the social effects of the pandemic by eliminating social barriers between students on university campuses and helping to improve social and mental conditions for students. We aim to develop a website to help connect and increase social interaction between not only students who live on campus, but also students who live off campus and commuter students. In our group of four, while one of us found it easy to make friends and meet new people as he was part of a “table tennis club," the other three of us struggled to make friends on campus. This motivated us to create a platform for students where they can meet other students with similar interests, as that would be an icebreaker.

DESIGN PROCESS

The first step was to identify users and start collecting data. Since it was for students of UIC, we decided to interview students pursuing different degrees, such as undergrad, graduate, and Ph.D. Another critical criterion was that we interviewed students who lived on campus, near campus, or commuted. It was to make sure we had the experience of students from all different backgrounds and reduce bias. We conducted personal interviews with eight students.

A few questions we asked our users to collect data are:
a. How has your experience been so far in making friends at the university? What challenges have you faced?
b. How different is your experience meeting new people and making friends at the university than at high school?
c. Are you a part of any clubs? 
            i.  If yes, ask how that has helped you to make friends.
            ii. If not, what is the reason behind not being a part of any clubs?

Through data collection, we were able to confirm that other students have faced social hardships at UIC during, and after the pandemic. It also gave us feedback and things to consider while creating our prototype.

Specific User Scenario

Sunny, an international student:
            Sunny is an international student from India who lives on campus with other Indian students. He is a master's student and primarily interacts with other international master's students, but he wants to meet other students who are like him and like to play Warzone. His roommate informs him about the application ‘Campus Buddy," which has a gaming community and allows other gamers on campus to meet each other. Sunny decides to create an account using his UIC UIN and password, and he puts on his profile that he is looking for gaming friends of all backgrounds. He joins the gaming page on ‘Campus Buddy’, and finds friends to play Warzone with. After playing the game together for a while, they decide to schedule a meeting using the meeting feature on "Campus Buddy," and they have dinner together near campus.

MY ROLE

I contributed to documentation, designing wireframes, doing multiple user studies, user scenarios, storyboarding, designing personas, and getting input from users on prototypes.

CONCLUSION AND LEARNINGS

We discovered from prior studies and our user study that students who participate in clubs or sports find it easier to meet other people and make new friends. As user needs and experiences differed slightly from what I initially believed user needs must be, we were once again reminded of the value of doing user studies.
