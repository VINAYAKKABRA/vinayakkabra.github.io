Rewardy

                           Earn passive income using debit card

My first exposure to HCI was during my undergraduate studies, and I was immediately drawn to the topic because it involved understanding human behavior for better design and it introduced me to Norman’s Design Principles. As a result, I opted for CS 422: User Interface Design and Programming when I had the option to choose courses offered at the University of Illinois, Chicago (UIC). I learned a lot of new concepts throughout the course, including personas, brainstorming, scenarios, focus groups, how to conduct them, affinity diagrams, etc. My final project for this coursework involved all these techniques.

INTRODUCTION

We had to redesign a solution from scratch for one of the startup ideas. We chose "Rewardy", a debit card that gives rewards when used, as our final project. As international students without SSNs, we are forced to use debit cards and thus miss out on cashback and rewards given by credit cards. This was our motivation to design a solution for the issue.

DESIGN PROCESS

To collect data, we conducted focus groups and personal interviews with a total of 9 users Some general questions we asked were about their preferences between credit cards and debit cards, their experiences, and their reasons for choosing one over the other.  Based on the data collected, we created an affinity diagram which can be seen below

SCENARIOS

One of the scenarios based on persona is:

Priyanka: Priyanka, an international student, is in the USA on an F1 visa. Currently, she doesn’t have any on-campus jobs. Thus, she attends lectures and is very active in a lot of different club activities. In her free time, she browses and shops on different shopping websites. As she is on an F1 visa and has no on-campus job, she can’t apply for SSN. As she has no SSN, she can’t apply for a credit card and is forced to use a normal debit card without rewards, but with our “Rewardy Debit Card”, she can shop and earn rewards. Being a shopaholic, she can spend without worrying about interest, debt, or her credit score, as she can only spend the amount that was deposited in her account. As there is no minimum balance requirement and a low balance fee, she can wait for a few days if there is a delay in her money order from her country without stressing about interest charges. Also, an easy and intuitive UI will show her account and transaction information. Furthermore, she explores a lot of new restaurants and shops from various brands. “Rewardy” provides discounts and offers that might interest her. A lot of features and rewards will tempt her to use a “Rewardy Debit Card” instead of a normal debit card with no rewards or offers.
